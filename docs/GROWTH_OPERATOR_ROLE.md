# Groundwork — Growth Operator Role: Armin

**For:** Armin
**From:** Ryan + Sameer
**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Source:** Founder working session, 2026-07-13

## The role in one line

Growth Operator: you own audience-building and top-of-funnel growth for Groundwork, full-time, starting now, organic-only, with one clear number to hit.

## Why this role exists

Groundwork is staying **organic-only until a $1M pre-seed raise**, triggered at 50,000 waitlist signups — no paid acquisition until then (see `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`). That means growth has to come from three things: content, direct outreach, and the referral loop built into the product. Ryan and Sameer are heads-down on the 60-day build sprint. This role is what makes sure growth isn't sitting idle while that happens — it runs in parallel, not after.

## What you own

### 1. Content and channel
- You're the face of Groundwork's public content — the primary channel is yours to run
- Content style: standout, funny, high-energy — not corporate, not a product pitch reel. Real cost breakdowns, contractor red flags, one link to the free estimate tool at the bottom, per the founder-led-content approach in `LAUNCH_PLAN.md`
- Ryan and Sameer appear in the waitlist launch video specifically (they're best positioned to describe the product after being deep in the build), and will show up naturally in day-to-day footage — but you're the one running the channel day to day, not them
- Waitlist landing page video (tracked as issue #18): cinematic, AI-generated, "Inside a Builder's Brain" concept, non-copyright audio, auto-plays into the signup form

### 2. Direct outreach
- Build and work the list: every real estate agency and private equity real estate group reachable via LinkedIn/email, added to a waitlist outreach pipeline
- Local press as a deliberate exception to organic-only (this is placement, not an ad buy) — county mail, a Washington Post/NYT slot — timing is a founder call, but you're the one who'd execute it

### 3. Referral loop adoption
- The product has a built-in growth mechanism (referral links, discount ladder — `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`) — your job is making sure early users actually know it exists and use it, not just that it's shipped in code
- This is the compounding piece: content and outreach get the first cohort, the referral loop is what's supposed to make each cohort bigger than the last

### 4. Founder feedback loop
- Weekly (or daily, early on) calls with early users alongside Ryan and Sameer — you're in the room for this, not just reading a summary after
- Direct user contact is how the content stays real instead of generic — the red flags and cost breakdowns you post should come from what you're actually hearing

## The number that matters

**~5,000 waitlist signups within the first 60 days** of the build sprint. That's not the finish line (the real trigger is 50,000, which is when a $1M pre-seed raise happens on our terms), but it's the checkpoint that tells us by day 60 whether the organic loop is actually working or needs a real channel change. Track this weekly, not just at the deadline — if it's off pace at day 20, that's the time to adjust, not day 55.

## What this role isn't

Product, backend, and Stripe/payments stay with Ryan and Sameer. If something you're hearing from users points to a real product gap, bring it to the weekly call — it goes through the same GitHub issue process as everything else (see `AGENT_HANDOFF.md`), it doesn't become a side build.

## The honest part

This is genuinely a "before it works" phase — realistic case is weeks or months of low or zero signal before the loop catches. That's not a sign it's broken, it's the normal shape of this kind of ramp: optimism now, a flatter and harder middle, then it either compounds or it doesn't. The ask is full commitment through the flat part, not just the parts that are fun to film. If something's going to pull focus — this needs to be the priority over it, not squeezed in around it, for the length of the 60-day sprint at minimum.

That's the deal: you own growth, you get real ownership of how it's told, and the one number above is how we'll both know if it's working.
