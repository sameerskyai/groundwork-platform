Sheet 02 / Problem
The Problem

Homeowners negotiate blind. Contractors pay to lose.

For the homeowner

  • No pricing reference point before a contractor walks in the door
  • Quotes for the same job vary wildly, with no way to tell fair from padded
  • Fear of being overcharged suppresses projects that would otherwise happen

For the contractor

  • Legacy lead platforms charge per lead, whether or not it closes
  • No cost signal means underbidding to win, then losing margin on the job
  • Every close creates the incentive to route the next job off-platform
Both sides are working from the same missing piece: a real number, before the conversation starts.

Product

One backend. Three front doors.

Homeowner, contractor, and realtor/property-manager portals share one matching engine — a percentage compatibility score, weighted differently per portal.
85%+
Minimum compatibility score to surface a match — budget, ZIP/distance, timeframe, personality
1
Matching engine, three weight profiles — homeowner, contractor, realtor / property manager
0
Phone numbers or addresses ever revealed between matched parties — in-app chat only, by design
Trust & safety is load-bearing, not a footnote: message filtering, a human-review flagging queue, and no address sharing anywhere in the product.

Go-To-Market

Organic until the number proves itself.

Sheet 12 / Risk
What Kills This

Five ways this fails, and the guardrail on each.

01
Contractor churn — leads that never become jobs. Guardrail: weekly lead-to-job tracking per contractor from day one.
02
Spreading thin — 30% density in ten metros beats nothing. Guardrail: one metro to 100% density before the next.
03
Estimate accuracy before the flywheel spins — one viral “we said $8K, it cost $30K” story. Guardrail: honest confidence labels until real data accumulates.
04
Trust incidents — one bad contractor story poisons a small launch market. Guardrail: manual verification before automation.
05
Losing the no-lead-fee discipline — a per-job cut would kill the entire differentiation. Guardrail: constitution-level rule, not a pricing knob.
