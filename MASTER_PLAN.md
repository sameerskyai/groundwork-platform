## 2. THE FIVE MECHANICS

### 2.1 The Home Passport

Every home builds a verified, permanent, timestamped record: every project, contractor, dollar, warranty, photo — tamper-evident. **The Passport transfers when the house sells.** Sellers get a more valuable house (Carfax'd homes sell better). Buyers inherit the home's full memory and are onboarded automatically. 6M US home sales/year = 6M built-in acquisition events. *"Carfax for homes — except we also do the repairs."*

### 2.2 The Home Health Score

Every house gets a 0–100 score that moves with maintenance behavior. Skip HVAC service → drops. Complete roof inspection → rises. Humans cannot ignore a number attached to something they own. Neighbors compare, buyers demand it, sellers flaunt it, insurers eventually pay for it. Retention becomes a game loop.

### 2.3 The Oracle

Predictive AI that speaks first: "Your water heater is 11 years old. In your ZIP these fail at year 12. Replace planned: ~$1,400. Replace flooded: ~$6,000. Want three quotes?" Fed by home age, systems data, regional patterns, and every completed job on the platform. **The data moat compounds daily.** You don't unsubscribe from the thing protecting your house.

### 2.4 The Neighborhood Nervous System

ZIP communities as collective intelligence: storm-response triggers ("3 roofs on your street replaced this month — free inspection?"), group-rate demand pooling ("7 neighbors need driveway sealing = 30% off"), true local price transparency from verified Graph data. Nobody has aggregated homeowner demand like a union.

### 2.5 The Financial Layer (Endgame — documented, not built)

Insurance discounts for high Health Scores. Groundwork-backed warranties. Equity intelligence ("these 3 projects raise your appraisal ~$40k"). The Passport becomes expected at sale the way inspections are. Built on top of verified home data at scale.

## 6. MARKET & FINANCIAL MODEL

### The TAM expansion

- **Old market:** 14M renovation events/year. Events end. Episodic revenue, churn by design.

- **New market:** ~145M owner-occupied US homes. Assets persist. Every home needs a passport, a score, and an oracle. **Thesis change expanded TAM ~10x and made revenue recurring instead of episodic.**

### Pricing

- Free: AI estimate, limited matching (1 active match / 3 reveals per day), community view, basic My Home

- **Homeowner+ $20/mo** ($10/mo permanent at 10 verified referrals): unlimited matching, full Graph/Passport, maintenance reminders, Home Team, unlimited Brain/Oracle, quote checker, Health Score detail, priority matching

- Contractor: free tier (1 lead/week) / $49/mo unlimited. Free during beta.

### The ceiling (internal fuel — see framing rules below)

- 5% of 145M homes = 7.25M subscribers × $20/mo = **$145M MRR ≈ $1.74B/year**

- Even at 5M subscribers: $100M MRR ≈ $1.2B/year

- Before contractor subs, referral-tier economics, or any Wave 5 revenue

### HOW TO TALK ABOUT THIS NUMBER (all three of us, always)

❌ Never pitch top-down: "if we capture 5% of 145M homes..." — this is the "1% of China" slide and it reads naive.

✅ Always pitch bottom-up: "Every home that completes ONE project gets a Passport, a Score, and an Oracle — and our retention thesis says they don't leave. 6M homes sell yearly and each sale transfers a Passport to a new owner. We don't need 5% of the market. We need one metro, a proven retention curve, and the transfer loop compounds. The ceiling happens to be 145M homes × $240/year — but the path is home by home, ZIP by ZIP."

### Unit economics (working assumptions, to be validated in beta)

- CAC target: referral-driven, <$20 blended

- LTV: $20/mo × multi-year retention (thesis: Graph makes churn structurally low) — even 24-month retention = $480 LTV

- The $10 referral tier is a CAC weapon, not lost revenue: 10 verified referrals per discount = we bought 10 users for $120/year of discount
